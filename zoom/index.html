Zoom is a remote conferencing services that combines video conferencing, online meetings, chat, and mobile collaboration.

  • SFAI is providing a Zoom account to all faculty to facilitate online class sessions
  • Zoom offers a web client and a desktop client
  • The desktop client has more features and is preferred for hosting classes.

When you first launch Zoom sign in with Google

Make sure to sign in with your SFAI account

You will be redirected to your web browser to login in, then Open Zoom.

In the opening Zoom window start with new meeting. We will talk about scheduling one a little bit later

After starting a new meeting you have the option of testing your audio setup. Afterwards choose 'Join With Computer Audio'

Hitting start video on the bottom toolbar will start the video session
The toolbar at the bottom of the Zoom window is important to understand so let walk through it's functions

Join Audio gives you the option to adjust your Mic and Speaker settings

The video button allows you to start, stop, adjust your video settings and use virtual backgrounds

The invite button allows you to invite participants

In the contact view, you will see the SFAI email directory listed.

On the email tab you can compose an email to send out from your default email client

You can also copy and paste the meeting URL or a more detailed invite and paste it into an email or document.
This is good for setting up quick meeting. In a little bit Patrick will present on how to set up recurring class meetings in google calendar and moodle

Your invitees will get an invitation like this

When they click on the link they will be prompted in install zoom if they have not already. If the zoom desktop client is installed they will be prompted to open it.

Invitees(students) do no need an account. Only the host(Teacher) needs to be logged in.
Guide for Students on How to Join a Zoom Meeting

Once the host enters the meeting all participants will appear.
  • It is suggested that you start your first class meeting and invite everyone ~15 minutes early.

Share screen allows you to share your screen with participants

You can choose to share your entire desktop

A shared whiteboard is also available

Or an individual window

When sharing your screen the toolbar will become a floating toolbar

Screen Annotation tools are also available when you are sharing your screen

The annotation tools allow you to draw on your screen, add text, show your mouse or highlight areas.
Further documentation on the annotation tools

Manage partipants allows gives you options for individual participants

You can mute individual audio or video

You can also promote them to the host, rename or remove them
Further documentation on managing participants

Chat opens a chat window on the side

You can post text and links here

At the bottom you can choose who you are chatting with, eveyone or individuals

You can also save the chat history and set options for whom others are allowed to chat with.
Further documentation on managing chat

Record allows you to record the meeting to either your computer or the Zoom cloud. You have limited storage on the Zoom cloud. Consider saving to you computer and sharing as a google drive link.
